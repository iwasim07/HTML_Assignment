Rain Droplets
  • Rain droplets are formed when water vapor in the atmosphere cools and condenses into tiny droplets of liquid water.
  • The size of rain droplets can vary from as small as 0.5 mm to as large as 6 mm in diameter.
  • The shape of rain droplets is not perfectly round, but rather somewhat flattened at the bottom and slightly bulging at the top due to air resistance.
